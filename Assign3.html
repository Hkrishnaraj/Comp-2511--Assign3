Work Experience


An internship allows you to try out various career positions and improve skills learned in school while studying to complete an academic program. Depending on your post-secondary program, completing an internship can also be a requirement for graduation. An internship is an introductory job position within an organization as part of a formal practicum or training program. Typically offered to undergraduate or graduate students, internships can offer a paid or unpaid position depending on the terms of employment and lasts for a specified period on a temporary basis. An internship provides a hands-on situation within a company to gain real-world work or research experience in your chosen field. Many companies use internship programs as an opportunity to recruit recent graduates and build their talent pool. Once the organization and intern have gotten to know one another, the company often extends permanent offers to internship candidates they saw potential in for current or future job openings.
